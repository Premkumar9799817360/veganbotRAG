RECIPE 1: CLASSIC OATMEAL WITH BERRIES
================================================================================
Servings: 2 | Prep Time: 2 min | Cook Time: 10 min | Total Time: 12 min

INGREDIENTS:
- 1 cup rolled oats (certified vegan)
- 2 cups water or unsweetened almond milk
- 1/2 cup fresh blueberries
- 1/2 cup fresh strawberries, sliced
- 1 tbsp maple syrup
- 1/2 tsp cinnamon
- Pinch of salt
- 2 tbsp walnuts, chopped
- 1 tbsp flaxseeds

COOKING INSTRUCTIONS:
1. In a medium saucepan, bring almond milk or water to a boil over medium heat.
2. Add rolled oats and a pinch of salt. Stir well.
3. Reduce heat to low and cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until oats are soft and creamy.
4. Stir in cinnamon and maple syrup.
5. Pour into bowls and top with blueberries, strawberries, walnuts, and flaxseeds.
6. Drizzle with extra maple syrup if desired.

SERVING SUGGESTIONS: Serve hot. Add extra plant milk on the side.

NUTRITION (per serving):
- Calories: 320
- Protein: 9g
- Carbohydrates: 52g
- Fat: 9g
- Fiber: 8g
- Sugar: 12g
- Iron: 15% DV
- Calcium: 20% DV (if using fortified almond milk)
- Vitamin C: 25% DV
- Omega-3: 1.8g

HEALTH BENEFITS: High in beta-glucan fiber for heart health, antioxidants from berries, 
anti-inflammatory omega-3 from walnuts and flaxseeds.

RECIPE 2: AVOCADO TOAST
================================================================================
Servings: 2 | Prep Time: 5 min | Cook Time: 3 min | Total Time: 8 min

INGREDIENTS:
- 4 slices whole grain bread (vegan)
- 2 ripe avocados
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- 1/4 tsp sea salt
- 1/4 tsp black pepper
- 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes
- 1 clove garlic (optional, for rubbing bread)
- 2 tbsp cherry tomatoes, halved
- 1 tbsp hemp seeds
- Fresh basil leaves for garnish
- Extra virgin olive oil drizzle

COOKING INSTRUCTIONS:
1. Toast bread slices until golden and crispy.
2. While bread toasts, halve avocados, remove pits, and scoop flesh into a bowl.
3. Add lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Mash to desired consistency (chunky or smooth).
4. Optional: Rub toasted bread with a cut garlic clove for extra flavor.
5. Spread avocado mixture generously on each slice.
6. Top with cherry tomatoes, hemp seeds, and red pepper flakes.
7. Drizzle with olive oil and garnish with fresh basil.

SERVING SUGGESTIONS: Serve immediately. Pair with orange juice or green tea.

NUTRITION (per serving - 2 slices):
- Calories: 390
- Protein: 10g
- Carbohydrates: 38g
- Fat: 24g (mostly healthy monounsaturated)
- Fiber: 12g
- Sugar: 3g
- Potassium: 720mg
- Folate: 30% DV
- Vitamin E: 15% DV
- Vitamin K: 28% DV

HEALTH BENEFITS: Heart-healthy fats, high fiber, folate-rich, anti-inflammatory.

RECIPE 3: SMOOTHIE BOWL
================================================================================
Servings: 1 | Prep Time: 5 min | Cook Time: 0 min | Total Time: 5 min

INGREDIENTS:
BASE:
- 1 frozen banana
- 1/2 cup frozen mango chunks
- 1/2 cup frozen pineapple
- 1/4 cup coconut milk (canned, full-fat for creaminess)
- 1/4 cup orange juice

TOPPINGS:
- 2 tbsp granola (vegan)
- 1/4 cup fresh berries
- 1 tbsp shredded coconut (unsweetened)

RECIPE 4: TOFU SCRAMBLE
================================================================================
Servings: 2 | Prep Time: 5 min | Cook Time: 12 min | Total Time: 17 min

INGREDIENTS:
- 400g (14 oz) firm tofu, pressed and drained
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1/2 onion, diced
- 1 red bell pepper, diced
- 1 cup baby spinach
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/4 tsp turmeric powder (for color)
- 1/4 tsp black salt (kala namak - gives egg-like flavor)
- 1/4 tsp black pepper
- 1/2 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp nutritional yeast
- 2 tbsp fresh chives or parsley, chopped
- 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved

COOKING INSTRUCTIONS:
1. Press tofu for at least 10 minutes using a tofu press or by wrapping in a clean towel and placing weight on top.
2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
3. Add onions and cook for 3 minutes until softened.
4. Add bell peppers and garlic, cook for 2 more minutes.
5. Crumble tofu into the pan with your hands, creating varied-sized pieces to mimic scrambled eggs.
6. Sprinkle turmeric, black salt, pepper, cumin, and nutritional yeast over tofu.
7. Stir and cook for 5-7 minutes until tofu is slightly golden.
8. Add spinach and cherry tomatoes, stir until spinach wilts (about 1 minute).
9. Taste and adjust seasoning. Garnish with fresh herbs.

SERVING SUGGESTIONS: Serve on toast, in a wrap, or with roasted potatoes.

NUTRITION (per serving):
- Calories: 280
- Protein: 22g
- Carbohydrates: 14g
- Fat: 16g
- Fiber: 4g
- Calcium: 35% DV
- Iron: 25% DV
- B12: 20% DV (from nutritional yeast)
- Zinc: 18% DV

HEALTH BENEFITS: High in plant protein, complete amino acids, isoflavones from soy, 
calcium-rich, anti-inflammatory turmeric.

RECIPE 5: CHIA SEED PUDDING
================================================================================
Servings: 2 | Prep Time: 5 min | Cook Time: 0 min | Chill Time: 4-8 hrs | Total Time: 4+ hrs

INGREDIENTS:
- 6 tbsp chia seeds
- 2 cups coconut milk (from carton, not canned)
- 2 tbsp maple syrup
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 1/4 tsp cinnamon

TOPPINGS:
- 1/2 cup fresh mango, diced
- 1/4 cup raspberries
- 2 tbsp toasted coconut flakes
- 1 tbsp pumpkin seeds
- Mint leaves for garnish

COOKING INSTRUCTIONS:
1. In a mixing bowl or mason jar, combine chia seeds, coconut milk, maple syrup, vanilla, and cinnamon.
2. Whisk well to prevent clumping.
3. Let sit for 5 minutes, then whisk again to break up any clusters.
4. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.
5. When ready to serve, stir pudding and adjust consistency (add more milk if too thick).
6. Spoon into bowls and add toppings: mango, raspberries, coconut flakes, pumpkin seeds.
7. Garnish with mint leaves.

MAKE-AHEAD: Prepare up to 4 days in advance and store in fridge.

NUTRITION (per serving):
- Calories: 340
- Protein: 8g
- Carbohydrates: 38g
- Fat: 18g
- Fiber: 14g
- Omega-3 ALA: 5g
- Calcium: 25% DV
- Phosphorus: 30% DV
- Magnesium: 35% DV

HEALTH BENEFITS: Exceptional omega-3 source, prebiotic fiber, blood sugar stabilizing, 
high in antioxidants, supports bone health.

RECIPE 6: VEGAN PANCAKES
================================================================================
Servings: 3 (about 9 medium pancakes) | Prep Time: 10 min | Cook Time: 20 min | Total Time: 30 min

INGREDIENTS:
DRY:
- 1.5 cups all-purpose flour (or whole wheat)
- 2 tsp baking powder
- 1/4 tsp salt
- 2 tbsp sugar
- 1 tsp cinnamon

WET:
- 1.5 cups unsweetened almond milk
- 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar (to make "buttermilk")
- 3 tbsp melted coconut oil
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 1 tbsp maple syrup

TOPPINGS:
- Maple syrup
- Fresh berries
- Sliced banana
- Coconut whipped cream (optional)

COOKING INSTRUCTIONS:
1. Mix almond milk and apple cider vinegar in a bowl. Let sit 5 minutes to curdle — this is your vegan buttermilk.
2. In a large bowl, whisk together all dry ingredients.
3. Add melted coconut oil, vanilla, and maple syrup to the buttermilk mixture. Stir.
4. Pour wet ingredients into dry ingredients. Mix gently until just combined — DO NOT OVERMIX. Lumps are fine!
5. Let batter rest for 3-5 minutes.
6. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Lightly oil with coconut oil.
7. Pour about 1/4 cup batter per pancake. Cook until bubbles form on surface and edges look set (2-3 min).
8. Flip and cook 1-2 more minutes until golden.
9. Serve immediately with toppings.

TIPS: For extra fluffy pancakes, don't skip the resting time. Keep finished pancakes warm in 200°F oven.

NUTRITION (per serving - 3 pancakes):
- Calories: 380
- Protein: 7g
- Carbohydrates: 58g
- Fat: 14g
- Fiber: 3g
- Iron: 15% DV
- Calcium: 20% DV

RECIPE 7: GRANOLA WITH PLANT MILK
================================================================================
Servings: 8 | Prep Time: 10 min | Cook Time: 30 min | Total Time: 40 min

INGREDIENTS (for granola):
- 3 cups rolled oats
- 1 cup mixed nuts (almonds, cashews, pecans), roughly chopped
- 1/2 cup pumpkin seeds
- 1/2 cup sunflower seeds
- 1/4 cup coconut flakes (unsweetened)
- 3 tbsp maple syrup
- 3 tbsp coconut oil, melted
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 1 tsp cinnamon
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1/2 cup dried cranberries or raisins (add AFTER baking)

TO SERVE (per bowl):
- 1/2 cup granola
- 1 cup oat milk or almond milk
- Fresh seasonal fruit

COOKING INSTRUCTIONS:
1. Preheat oven to 325°F (165°C). Line a large baking sheet with parchment.
2. In a large bowl, mix oats, nuts, seeds, and coconut flakes.
3. Add maple syrup, melted coconut oil, vanilla, cinnamon, and salt. Toss until evenly coated.
4. Spread in a thin, even layer on baking sheet.
5. Bake for 25-30 minutes, stirring halfway through, until golden brown.
6. Remove from oven. Let cool COMPLETELY without stirring (this creates clusters!).
7. Mix in dried cranberries once cooled.
8. Store in airtight container for up to 2 weeks.
9. Serve with plant milk and fresh fruit.

NUTRITION (per serving):
- Calories: 420
- Protein: 11g
- Carbohydrates: 48g
- Fat: 22g
- Fiber: 6g
- Vitamin E: 25% DV
- Magnesium: 28% DV
- Zinc: 20% DV

RECIPE 8: OVERNIGHT OATS
================================================================================
Servings: 1 | Prep Time: 5 min | Chill Time: 8 hrs | Total Time: 8+ hrs

INGREDIENTS:
- 1/2 cup rolled oats
- 3/4 cup oat milk (or any plant milk)
- 1 tbsp chia seeds
- 1 tbsp maple syrup
- 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
- 1/4 tsp cinnamon
- Pinch of salt

TOPPINGS (morning):
- 1/2 banana, sliced
- 1/4 cup fresh blueberries
- 1 tbsp almond butter
- 1 tsp hemp seeds
- Splash of extra plant milk

COOKING INSTRUCTIONS:
1. In a mason jar or container, combine oats, oat milk, chia seeds, maple syrup, vanilla, cinnamon, and salt.

6. Enjoy cold, or microwave for 1-2 minutes if preferred warm.

RECIPE 9: VEGAN FRENCH TOAST
================================================================================
Servings: 2 | Prep Time: 8 min | Cook Time: 15 min | Total Time: 23 min

INGREDIENTS:
- 4 thick slices bread (brioche-style vegan bread or sourdough)
- 1 cup canned full-fat coconut milk
- 2 tbsp chickpea flour (acts as egg binder)
- 1 tbsp nutritional yeast
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 1 tsp cinnamon
- 1/4 tsp nutmeg
- 1 tbsp maple syrup
- 1 tbsp coconut oil (for cooking)

TOPPINGS:

- Powdered sugar (vegan)

RECIPE 10: BANANA NICE CREAM
================================================================================
Servings: 2 | Prep Time: 5 min (plus freezing) | Cook Time: 0 min | Total Time: 5 min active

INGREDIENTS:
- 4 large ripe bananas, peeled, sliced, and frozen overnight
- 2 tbsp peanut butter
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 2 tbsp cacao nibs or vegan chocolate chips
- Pinch of salt

TOPPINGS:
- Sliced fresh banana
- Granola
- Drizzle of peanut butter
- Cacao nibs

COOKING INSTRUCTIONS:
1. Freeze banana slices in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a bag. Freeze 8+ hours.
2. Place frozen banana pieces in a food processor or high-speed blender.
3. Process, scraping sides often, until creamy and smooth (3-4 minutes). It will first look crumbly, 
   then suddenly transform into a creamy ice cream texture.
4. Add peanut butter, vanilla, and salt. Pulse to combine.
5. Fold in cacao nibs.
6. Serve immediately as soft-serve or freeze 30 more minutes for firmer texture.
7. Scoop into bowls and add toppings.

NUTRITION (per serving):
- Calories: 310
- Protein: 8g
- Carbohydrates: 54g
- Fat: 10g
- Fiber: 6g
- Potassium: 750mg
- Vitamin B6: 40% DV
- Magnesium: 20% DV

RECIPE 13: VEGAN WAFFLES
================================================================================
Servings: 4 (8 waffles) | Prep Time: 10 min | Cook Time: 25 min | Total Time: 35 min

INGREDIENTS:
- 2 cups all-purpose flour
- 2 tsp baking powder
- 1/2 tsp baking soda
- 1/4 tsp salt
- 2 tbsp sugar
- 2 cups oat milk
- 2 tsp apple cider vinegar
- 1/4 cup melted coconut oil
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 1 tbsp flaxseed meal mixed with 3 tbsp water (flax egg)

COOKING INSTRUCTIONS:
1. Make flax egg: combine flaxseed meal and water, stir, and let sit 5 minutes.
2. Make vegan buttermilk: combine oat milk and apple cider vinegar, let sit 5 minutes.

5. Add wet to dry ingredients. Stir until just combined — some lumps are fine!
6. Preheat and grease waffle iron.
7. Pour batter into waffle iron according to manufacturer instructions.
8. Cook until deep golden and crispy (usually 4-5 minutes).
9. Serve with maple syrup, fresh fruit, and coconut whipped cream.

NUTRITION (per serving - 2 waffles):
- Calories: 420

RECIPE 15: BLACK BEAN BREAKFAST BURRITO
================================================================================

INGREDIENTS:
- 2 large flour tortillas (vegan)
- 1 can (400g) black beans, drained and rinsed
- 1 red bell pepper, diced
- 1/2 red onion, diced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp chili powder
- 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 avocado, sliced
- 1/4 cup salsa
- 2 tbsp fresh cilantro
- 1 lime, juiced
- 1/4 cup vegan cheese shreds (optional)

7. Lay tortilla flat. Add bean mixture down the center.
8. Top with avocado slices, salsa, cilantro, and vegan cheese.

RECIPE 30: MATCHA LATTE
================================================================================
Servings: 1 | Prep Time: 3 min | Cook Time: 5 min | Total Time: 8 min

INGREDIENTS:
- 1 tsp ceremonial grade matcha powder
- 2 tbsp hot water (not boiling, about 175°F/80°C)
- 1 cup oat milk (or other plant milk)
- 1-2 tsp maple syrup or agave
- 1/4 tsp vanilla extract (optional)

COOKING INSTRUCTIONS:

2. Add 2 tbsp hot water. Whisk vigorously in a zigzag motion until smooth paste, no lumps.

4. For a frothy latte: use a hand frother or steam milk.
5. Add maple syrup and vanilla to matcha paste. Stir.
6. Pour frothed oat milk into matcha, stirring gently.
7. For iced version: add ice, cold milk, then pour matcha shot over.

- Fortified plant milks (almond, oat, soy)
- Tahini (sesame paste), chia seeds
- Kale, bok choy, collard greens
- Fortified tofu (with calcium sulfate)
- Teff grain, amaranth
- Tempeh

- Lentils, chickpeas, black beans
- Tofu, tempeh, soybeans
- Pumpkin seeds, hemp seeds
- Quinoa, oats, amaranth
- Dark leafy greens (kale, spinach)
- TIP: Eat iron-rich foods with Vitamin C to triple absorption!

OMEGA-3 FATTY ACIDS:
- Flaxseeds and flaxseed oil (best plant source ALA)
- Chia seeds, hemp seeds, walnuts
- Note: For EPA/DHA, consider algae-based omega-3 supplements

- Nutritional yeast (fortified)
- Fortified plant milks
- Fortified breakfast cereals
- B12 supplement recommended for all vegans

VITAMIN D:
- Fortified plant milks and orange juice
- Mushrooms exposed to sunlight
- Supplements strongly recommended (most people are deficient)

ZINC:
- Pumpkin seeds, hemp seeds
- Legumes (chickpeas, lentils)
- Nuts and seeds
- Whole grains

IODINE:
- Seaweed and nori (can be high — don't overdo)
- Iodized salt
- Supplement often recommended for vegans
